In brief

A decade of building rooms, then building the systems behind them.

I started as a videographer and event photographer in DC nightlife. Along the way I became a community organizer almost by accident — running a monthly event series, building a media platform to 220K followers, and watching, year after year, the same pattern: Black creative talent generates the energy a room sells tickets on, and is the last to get paid for it, if it gets paid at all.

ATCHÉ is the answer I'm building. A platform where creative women in nightlife — photographers, hosts, DJs, stylists, content creators — get discovered, booked, and paid on time, backed by real infrastructure: venue certification, revenue attribution, and a compensation model that pays first and treats access as a bonus, not a substitute.

I built this for women like my sister — talented, underestimated, and deserving of a system that actually protects them instead of just using them.
