Product — ATCHÉ

Nightlife runs on Black creative women.
They rarely run it.

ATCHÉ is a community-first platform for the photographers, hosts, DJs, stylists, and content creators who power nightlife — built to get them discovered, booked, paid on time, and protected. Launching in Washington, DC through the HBCU network, then Atlanta, Baltimore, and Charlotte.

ConceptThe network underneath the night.
The problem

The economic engine of a scene is rarely the one collecting the receipts.

Black creative women generate enormous cultural and economic value in nightlife — they're the reason rooms are full, content travels, and venues build a following. But the industry around them has almost no infrastructure: no standard contracts, no guaranteed payment timelines, no system for proving their value to a venue or brand, and no real protection if a booking goes wrong.

That gap isn't a talent problem. It's an infrastructure problem. ATCHÉ exists to close it.

Who it's for

Three tiers, one trust system.

The Circle
Creative women — invite & application only

Photographers, hosts, DJs, stylists, and content creators. The Circle is the core of ATCHÉ — vetted, supported, and positioned to get booked and paid fairly. Membership is earned, not bought.

The Network
Brand & venue partners

Venues and brands that want access to a vetted creative bench, certified standards, and measurable results — through sponsorships, activations, and certified partnership status.

The Room
Guests

A premium membership layer for guests who want curated access to the events and spaces The Circle is shaping — a loyalty layer that funds the system without compromising it.

How it actually works

This isn't a booking app. It's accountability infrastructure.

01

Venue certification, Michelin-style

Venues earn and maintain an ATCHÉ rating by meeting a real standard — treatment of artists, payment terms, safety, and representation. The certification becomes a credential venues want, and a paid product ATCHÉ can sell back to them.

02

Revenue attribution

The ability to track the revenue a specific artist or performer drives for a venue — turning anecdotal "she brings a crowd" into a number that justifies better pay, better billing, and better booking fees.

03

Native artist booking

Booking happens inside the platform, not through a DM and a prayer. ATCHÉ is the transaction layer, not just the discovery layer — which is what makes payment terms enforceable in the first place.

04

Artist commentary & community-validated ratings

Artists can publicly review the venues they work — not just the other way around. That gives the rating system credibility and gives creative women a voice in which rooms get to call themselves reputable.

Compensation philosophy

Pay first. Perks second.

ATCHÉ's core differentiator is simple: fair payment is the baseline, not the reward. Access, education, and status are recruitment tools that sit on top of real pay — never a substitute for it. That's a deliberate stance in an industry where "exposure" has too often been the excuse for not paying creative women what they're worth.

"I built this for women like my sister — talented enough to carry a room, and too often underpaid for it. ATCHÉ is what I wish already existed for her."

Xavier Snow, Founder
Rollout

Built for one city first, designed for many.

Phase 1

Launch in Washington, DC, anchored in the Howard University and HBCU network — founding members of The Circle, first venue partners, and an organic, verifiable growth strategy (no purchased followers, no shortcuts).

Phase 2

Expand to Atlanta, Baltimore, and Charlotte — cities with the same density of HBCU networks, nightlife culture, and untapped creative talent that DC has.

Phase 3

Layer in brand activations, certified venue partnerships, and a creator/UGC track so Circle members can earn from brand and ambassador work beyond live events.
